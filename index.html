About me

I build things with data that make people go "how did we not see this?"

My journey started in electronics and telecom engineering in Nagpur, India — which gave me an instinct for systems thinking and precision. But it was data that captured me completely. I moved to the US to pursue my MS at University of North Texas, and haven't looked back since.

At American Airlines, I got to work on something genuinely hard: predicting baggage delays from 50 million records. Building that pipeline from scratch — cleaning, modeling, validating — and watching it translate into a 12% reduction in late baggage incidents is still one of my proudest moments.

Before that, three years at Accenture taught me how enterprise data actually works in the wild — messy, high-stakes, and deeply political. I cut SQL query times by 30–40% and learned that the best data work is invisible: it just makes everything downstream faster and more reliable.

Right now I'm at Bright Mind Enrichment, building LLM-driven automation and real-time dashboards. I love that data lets me touch every part of a business — and I'm always looking for the next interesting problem.
